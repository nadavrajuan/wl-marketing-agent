# Partner and Market Research Playbook

## Goal

Give the future agent a dedicated process for researching:

- partners
- competitors
- market themes
- Reddit / community sentiment
- deep-link opportunities

This is separate from campaign analytics because the meeting made it clear that success here depends on broad market intelligence, not only internal ad data.

## Part 1: Partner Research Objectives

For every partner or provider, the agent should learn:

- what it sells
- how it positions itself
- who it is for
- whether it emphasizes branded/original products, compounded products, or a mix
- how price-forward it is
- how trust-forward it is
- how broad its support model is
- how its flow works
- how credible / stable / scalable it appears
- how its economics compare to alternatives

## Part 2: Partner Dossier Template

Each partner should have a structured dossier with:

- partner name
- official domain
- current status
- internal source network
- offer summary
- medication / product summary
- original vs compounded notes
- pricing notes
- onboarding flow notes
- support model notes
- trust / legitimacy notes
- likely audience fit
- internal conversion notes
- internal payout notes
- observed changes over time
- risk flags
- open questions
- last verified date

## Part 3: Research Sources Per Partner

The agent should gather evidence from:

- the live comparison site
- partner official site
- review pages on the live site
- partner legal / FAQ / support pages
- public pricing pages
- regulatory / official sources where relevant
- community chatter
- competitor comparisons
- internal campaign and conversion data

## Part 5: Partner Scoring Framework

The agent should score partners across:

- purchase efficiency
- payout attractiveness
- offer clarity
- trust / legitimacy
- compliance risk
- price competitiveness
- support depth
- speed / friction
- historical stability
- strategic priority

## Part 6: Competitor Monitoring Workflow

The meeting strongly suggested that competitors are ahead and can teach faster than trial-and-error alone.

The competitor workflow should monitor:

- homepage changes
- comparison-page changes
- ranking changes
- price changes
- CTA changes
- partner additions/removals
- medication emphasis changes
- claim-language changes

### Competitor monitoring cycle

Daily:

- detect visible page diffs on monitored domains

Weekly:

- summarize strategic changes

Monthly:

- compare pattern shifts across competitors

## Part 7: Deep-Link Discovery Workflow

The team explicitly wants not just public homepages, but the campaign-relevant pages.

The agent should try to discover:

- landing pages used in ads
- compare pages beneath the homepage
- tracking links
- variant pages
- likely paid-media pages discovered through:
  - SERP exploration
  - internal site crawling
  - public ad libraries where available
  - URL pattern expansion
  - crawl-path heuristics

The agent should mark findings by confidence:

- confirmed campaign page
- likely campaign page
- possible campaign page

## Part 8: Reddit / Community Research Workflow

The goal is not generic social listening.

The goal is early-signal detection.

The agent should track:

- brand mentions
- ingredient mentions
- trust/safety concerns
- pricing concerns
- delivery issues
- side-effect themes
- approval confusion
- sentiment changes

The agent should look for:

- rising complaints about a partner
- rising praise for a partner
- emerging medication / ingredient interest
- language users naturally use when describing needs
- themes that precede changes in conversion behavior

## Part 9: Community Research Output

Each community scan should return:

- top discussed partners
- top complaint themes
- top trust themes
- top pricing themes
- emerging keyword ideas
- risk alerts
- confidence rating

## Part 10: Market Theme Research

Beyond individual partners, the agent should maintain a market-theme layer:

- price sensitivity
- trust / safety sensitivity
- compounded vs branded language shifts
- holistic-care vs medication-only positioning
- speed / approval / delivery expectations
- support expectations

## Part 11: Warning System

The future system should generate warnings for:

- partner suddenly disappearing or becoming inactive
- competitor changing rankings dramatically
- major price move
- new compliance-sensitive language on the live site
- large rise in negative sentiment for a partner
- strong new competitor angle
- internal winner suddenly degrading

## Part 12: What Success Looks Like

This playbook is successful if the agent can answer:

- Which partners deserve more traffic right now?
- Which partners should be deprioritized?
- Which competitors are teaching us something important?
- Which market themes are rising?
- Which landing pages or angles are missing from our current strategy?
- Which signals require immediate human review?
